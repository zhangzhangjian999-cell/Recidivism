Data availability 
The Ukrainian recidivism dataset used in this study includes individual records. It was obtained through a formal research agreement with the State Penitentiary Service of Ukraine. The dataset was constructed from administrative records covering multiple correctional institutions across different regions of Ukraine and includes demographic, criminal, and sentencing information. The data are not publicly available because they include sensitive personal information. They are protected by national privacy laws and GDPR rules. Researchers can request access through their institutions. The process requires ethics committee approval, a data use agreement, and permission from the Ukrainian authority. Formal procedures and contact points for data access are available via the Ukrainian Ministry of Justice portal (https://minjust.gov.ua).
The North Carolina historical prison release data are taken from the dataset titled Predicting Recidivism in North Carolina. This dataset is publicly available through the United States federal open data catalog (catalog.data.gov) at https://catalog.data.gov/dataset/predicting-recidivism-in-north-carolina-1978-and-1980-13099.
The Bureau of Justice Statistics maintains the NCRP data. Researchers can access it by submitting a formal request on the program page (https://bjs.ojp.gov/data-collection/national-corrections-reporting-program-ncrp).
